Ghostriding

Michael Carl Tschantz, Mary McGlohon, Carsten Varming, Jennifer Marlow, Jeffrey Stylos, Stacey Kuznetsov

March 30, 2009

Audio-visual material

Abstract: Ghost-riding, frequently used in the context of "ghost-riding the whip" (a "whip" being a vehicle) or simply ghostin', is when a person puts a car with an automatic transmission in drive or allows it to idle and then the driver (and passengers) of a vehicle exit while it is still rolling and dance beside it or on the hood or roof. (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghost-riding)
