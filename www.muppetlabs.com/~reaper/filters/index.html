Text Filters

Somewhere along the way, I started collecting lex text filters. These are commandline filters you can use in Unix, which will take incoming text, and spit out.. "modified" text. They might work in DOS as well, but I'm not sure how you'd do it.

NOTE: if you are offended by non-PC attitudes and assumptions, do not read on! These filters can be offensive by today's standards. While it's not my intention to shock or dismay anyone, I'm also not going to prettify reality. And the reality is that someone wrote each one of these filters, most likely because they thought it was funny. I think they're funny too.

For the purposes of the table below, I'll modify the following text with each of the filters:

Four score and seven years ago our fathers brought forth on this continent, a new nation, conceived in liberty, and dedicated to the proposition that all men are created equal.

Now we are engaged in a great civil war, testing whether that nation, or any nation so conceived and so dedicated, can long endure. We are met on a great battlefield of that war. We have come to dedicate a portion of that field, as a final resting place for those who here gave their lives that that nation might live. It is altogether fitting and proper that we should do this.

Kraut I think this filter is just a general mockery of the way a native German speaker speaks English -- a long-time favorite of cartoon villians and movie badguys. I disagree with at least one aspect of this filter: native German speakers tend to say "zis" instead of "dis" when pronouncing the word "this." (At least, in Hamburg they did.)

Newspeak Newspeak is my absolute favorite filter of the bunch. The others are fun, but this is a work of genius, and it's nowhere near done. Newspeak is the language that Orwell proposed the State would create and push, in his book "1984". Aside from being a wonderful work of fiction and well worth a read, the language that Orwell proposes is very interesting. This filter tends to work best on political speeches and announcements.

This filter needs some improvement to deal with special cases which are usually spelled one way ("phreak", "war3z", "d00d", etc.).
