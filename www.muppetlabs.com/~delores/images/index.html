  • Seattle Marathon 2000
    Phoebe and I ran the Seattle Half Marathon this year. It was cold, it was rainy, it was damn early in the morning, and it was 13.1 miles. This picture was taken about 10 or 20 feet in front of the finish line. I'm sure Phoebe will fondly recall wanting to kill me because nearing the end, I grabbed her hand and starting sprinting towards the finish line. [In the photo, you can actually tell the difference between our gait and the people around us.] Endorphins will make a girl do funny things.

    Wintertime, and a young girl's heart turns to full-auto machine guns. The December 2000 shoot was damned fine fun - got to shoot a Lahti, one of the 20mm Finnish anti-tank cannons immortalized in Ross' "Unintended Consequences," the Atlas Shrugged of the gun culture in more ways than one.
